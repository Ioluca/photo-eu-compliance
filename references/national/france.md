# France — National Photography Law Reference

## Key Legislation
- **Code Civil Art. 9** — Droit à l'image (Right to image)
- **Code de la Propriété Intellectuelle** — Copyright
- **Loi Informatique et Libertés** (as amended for GDPR alignment)

---

## Droit à l'image (Art. 9 Code Civil)

France has one of the strongest image rights protections in Europe.

**Principle:** "Chacun a droit au respect de sa vie privée" — Everyone has
the right to respect for their private life. Courts have extended this to
cover the right to one's own image.

**Consent requirement:** Photographing and publishing a recognizable person's
image requires their **prior, specific consent** — for each distinct use.

**Exceptions:**
- Public figures in the exercise of their public functions
- Events of current news interest (droit à l'information)
- Public gatherings where no individual is singled out
- Artistic necessity (narrow interpretation)

**Key jurisprudence:**
- Consent for one use does not extend to other uses
- A model release for a magazine does not cover social media
- Publication without consent can result in damages (€1,000-50,000+ typical)
- Even public figures retain image rights in private settings

## GDPR Implementation
- **CNIL** (French DPA) is one of the most active in Europe
- Photography-specific guidance from CNIL exists for events and public spaces
- Art. 85 derogation implemented broadly for journalism, more narrowly for art
- AI enforcement: CNIL has been active on AI data protection issues
