# Italy — National Photography Law Reference

## Key Legislation
- **Legge 633/1941** (Legge sul Diritto d'Autore — LDA)
- **Art. 10 Codice Civile** (Diritto all'immagine)
- **D.Lgs. 196/2003** (Codice Privacy, as amended by D.Lgs. 101/2018 for GDPR alignment)
- **Provvedimenti del Garante Privacy** (Data Protection Authority rulings)

---

## Right to Image (Diritto all'immagine)

### Art. 10 Codice Civile
The image of a person cannot be displayed, reproduced, or commercially
exploited without their consent, unless justified by their notoriety,
public office, necessity of justice or police, or scientific/educational
purposes, provided the display does not prejudice their honor or reputation.

### LDA Art. 96-97

**Art. 96:** The portrait of a person cannot be displayed, reproduced, or
commercially exploited without the consent of the subject.

**Art. 97 — Exceptions (no consent needed when):**
1. The person is well-known (notoriety, public office, etc.)
2. The photo is connected to facts, events, or ceremonies of public interest
3. The photo is taken in a public context
4. **BUT:** Even in these cases, the photo cannot harm the honor, reputation,
   or decorum of the person depicted

### Practical interpretation for photographers

| Scenario | Consent needed? | Legal basis |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| Street photography in piazza | Generally no, if in public context | Art. 97(1) LDA |
| Individual portrait on the street (close-up, identifiable) | Yes, recommended | Art. 96 LDA + GDPR |
| Public event (concert, demonstration, sports) | No for the event; yes if isolating individuals | Art. 97 LDA |
| Wedding guests (hired as photographer) | Covered by contract with couple; notify guests | Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR |
| Commercial use of street photo | Yes, always | Art. 96 LDA |
| Editorial/journalistic use | No, if newsworthy | Art. 97 + Art. 85 GDPR derogation |

## GDPR Implementation — Italian Specifics

### Codice Privacy (D.Lgs. 196/2003, as amended)

**Art. 136-139 — Journalism and artistic expression derogation:**
Processing of personal data for journalistic purposes or artistic/literary
expression is exempt from several GDPR obligations, including:
- Prior consent requirement (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR)
- Some data subject rights
- Purpose limitation (partially)

**Condition:** The journalist/artist must still respect the essential dignity
of the person and act within the Deontological Code for Journalism
(Codice Deontologico dei Giornalisti) or analogous professional standards.

**Garante Privacy rulings relevant to photography:**
- Street photography generally falls under legitimate interest or artistic
  expression, but individual close-ups in compromising situations require consent
- Event photographers should provide notice (signage or verbal) to attendees
- Social media publication of photos with identifiable third parties has been
  sanctioned when the subject objected

### AI-Specific (Italy)

Italy has not yet adopted specific national legislation on AI beyond the
EU AI Act. The existing privacy framework applies. The Garante has been
active in AI enforcement (e.g., temporary ChatGPT ban in 2023) and is
expected to provide guidance on AI-generated content in photography.

## Stock Photography — Italian Tax Considerations

Italian photographers selling stock internationally should be aware:
- Income from stock sales is typically classified as "reddito da lavoro autonomo"
- VAT (IVA) applies to sales within the EU
- Cross-border platform payments may require proper invoicing
- This is a tax matter, not a compliance matter — consult a commercialista
