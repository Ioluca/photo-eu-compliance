# AI Disclosure Contract Clause — Templates

## Variant A — Light (Wedding / Portrait)

### AI-Assisted Post-Production Disclosure

The Photographer employs professional post-production techniques that may
include AI-assisted tools for image enhancement, such as noise reduction,
color optimization, and minor retouching. These tools enhance the natural
quality of the images and do not alter the fundamental content or likeness
of the subjects.

The Photographer commits to delivering images that accurately represent
the moments captured during the session/event.

**Section 2 — Client Acknowledgment**
The Client acknowledges and accepts that the above AI-assisted tools are
part of the Photographer's standard professional workflow and that the
resulting images may contain AI-modified or AI-generated elements.

**Section 3 — Downstream Disclosure Responsibility**
The Client is informed that under Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (AI Act),
particularly Article 50, content that has been substantially generated
or manipulated by AI may require disclosure when made publicly available.

When publishing images that contain substantial AI-generated content
(as identified by the Photographer), the Client is responsible for:
- Preserving image metadata (IPTC, C2PA Content Credentials)
- Complying with applicable transparency requirements in their jurisdiction
- Including appropriate disclosures in advertising materials if required

The Photographer will inform the Client which images, if any, contain
substantial AI-generated content requiring downstream disclosure.

**Section 4 — Metadata**
The Photographer will embed appropriate metadata in all delivered images,
including IPTC Digital Source Type classification reflecting the production
method used.

## Variant C — Strict (Editorial / Journalism)

### AI Usage Policy — Editorial Integrity

**Section 1 — AI Transparency Commitment**
The Photographer commits to full transparency regarding any AI tools
used in the production of editorial/journalistic images.

**Section 2 — Permitted AI Tools**
The following AI-assisted tools are used and considered standard
post-production processing:
- AI noise reduction
- AI-assisted selection/masking for exposure and color work
- Lens correction and perspective adjustment

**Section 3 — Restricted AI Tools**
The following AI tools will NOT be used on editorial/journalistic images
without explicit prior written agreement from the Client:
- Generative content removal or addition
- AI sky replacement
- Generative frame expansion
- Any tool that creates pixels not present in the original scene
- Face modification or enhancement beyond standard retouching

**Section 4 — Mandatory Disclosure**
If any generative AI tools are used with Client approval, the resulting
images will be:
- Labeled in IPTC metadata with Digital Source Type
  "compositeWithTrainedAlgorithmicMedia"
- Accompanied by C2PA Content Credentials documenting all modifications
- Clearly identified in the delivery notes as AI-modified
- Captioned as "[AI-Modified]" or equivalent in any publication

**Section 5 — Compliance**
Both parties agree to comply with:
- Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (AI Act), Article 50
- IPTC Photo Metadata Standard recommendations
- Applicable editorial ethics codes (NPPA, Reuters, AP, national equivalents)
- Directive (EU) 2019/790 copyright provisions

**Section 6 — Liability**
The Photographer assumes responsibility for accurately disclosing AI usage.
The Client assumes responsibility for maintaining disclosures in publication.
